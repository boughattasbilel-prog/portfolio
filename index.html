— Engineering Challenges & Solutions
Challenge 1
High current + compact form factor
Contact temperature exceeded 150 °C under rated load due to current concentration. Localized heating caused creep deformation in the PBT housing around the contact zone. Insufficient thermal path to ambient.
Fixed
Multi-contact architecture + copper inlay
Redesigned to symmetric multi-contact layout for even current distribution. Added copper inlay to conduct heat away. Introduced 0.5 mm air gap with TIM between contact and polymer. Reduced contact temperature to 95 °C under rated load.
Challenge 2
PBT thermal cycling failure (−40 → +150 °C, 500 cycles)
Differential expansion between PBT and metal contacts accumulated 3.2 mm cumulative displacement over 500 cycles. Original LCP housing was brittle below −20 °C. Looseness and contact wear measured at 250 cycles. 5% shrinkage scatter on contact bore diameter consumed the entire ±0.15 mm stack-up budget.
Fixed
LCP → PBT-GF30 transition + stress-relief ribs + pack-hold optimization
Reduced wall thickness from 2.8 mm to 2.4 mm to lower CTE mismatch stress. Added stress-relief ribs at critical junctions. Implemented elastic retention clips. Optimized pack-hold profile — shrinkage scatter reduced to <1%. Passed IEC 61076-2-109 thermal cycling 500 cycles.
Challenge 3
Injection molding at 500 K/year volume
Initial design had undercuts requiring mechanical mold extraction — cycle time 65+ s. Porosity appeared in thin-wall sections. First tooling trial showed 8% defect rate. Snap-fit failed 50 N pull-force test at first iteration due to insufficient deflection geometry.
Fixed
Parting line redesign + Moldflow + conformal cooling
Eliminated undercuts via smart parting line placement. Optimized gate locations in Moldflow CAE. Implemented conformal cooling channels (Ø 3.5 mm). Redesigned snap-fit with 15% additional deflection clearance. Reduced cycle time to <45 s. Achieved 80 units/min at <2% defect rate.

3D CAD & drawings: SolidWorks modeling, technical drawings per ISO 13715, GD&T per ASME Y14.5. Managed revisions through ECO process.

Tolerance stack-up: Worst-case and statistical (RSS) analysis. Critical dims: ±0.1 mm on contact seating surface, ±0.05 mm on pin insertion depth.

Validation: Supervised thermal cycling, electrical testing, creepage/clearance per IEC 60664-1, vibration per ISO 16750-3 Cat. 3H.

Y-Quad —
Four-Contact 200 A Battery Interconnect

Four-contact battery module connector for 48 V / 12 V hybrid platforms. Managed the LCP → PBT material transition that cut unit cost by 29.8% (€4.20 → €2.95) while meeting all thermal cycling requirements. Resolved fretting corrosion on aluminum contacts by increasing spring force from 15 N to 22 N. Calculated creepage distance to 4 mm minimum per IEC 60664-1 Pollution Degree 3.

—Engineering Challenges & Solutions
Challenge 1
LCP-to-PBT material transition — shrinkage & flow mismatch
PBT has lower melt flow (MFR 8 vs. 15 for LCP) and higher shrinkage (1.8% vs. 0.8%). First PBT shots showed 5% shrinkage variation on contact bore diameter — directly consuming the entire ±0.15 mm stack-up budget. Wall geometry optimized for LCP was incompatible with PBT fill.
Fixed
Wall thickness + pack-hold optimization → €2.95/unit (−29.8%)
Increased nominal wall from 1.6 mm to 2.0 mm to reduce shear rate and shrinkage scatter. Added cooling ribs. Optimized cavity pressure pack-hold profile via injection process study. Shrinkage scatter reduced to <0.8%. Final cost: €2.95/unit. Passed IEC 61076 thermal cycling.
Challenge 2
Fretting corrosion on Al contacts — 2.1% resistance drift at 10 K cycles
2.1% contact resistance drift measured after 10 K mate/unmate cycles in humid environment. Root cause: spring force of 15 N insufficient to prevent micro-slip at contact interface under ISO 16750-3 vibration. Confirmed by SEM analysis of contact surfaces showing micro-plowing patterns.
Fixed
Spring force +7 N + selective Au plating + parylene-C coating
Increased spring force from 15 N to 22 N. Applied selective Au plating 0.3 µm on contact tips with silver-filled underlayer for lubricity. Added parylene-C conformal coating (10 µm) on spring elements. Result: <0.5% resistance drift over 50 K cycles in salt-fog test.
—Role & Responsibilities

Material transition study: Full LCP → PBT evaluation — flow, shrinkage, CTE, thermal cycling performance. Validated thermal/mechanical performance against OEM specs.
TOL
4-contact assembly stack-up: ±0.1 mm contact alignment guaranteed across 4-position layout. Worst-case and RSS methods both verified.
FME
DFMEA: 8 critical failure modes identified. Spring force, contact plating, housing wall thickness identified as key control parameters.
MOL
Injection process optimization: Gate locations, cooling channels, cycle time <50 s. Pack-hold profile verified via cavity pressure sensors.
VAL
Testing supervision: Contact resistance, dielectric strength, 50 K cycle reliability, thermal cycling validation (IEC 61076).

—Engineering Challenges & Solutions
Challenge 1
Impedance control — ±8 Ω scatter on PTFE insulator
±0.2 mm PTFE insulator thickness variation directly shifts characteristic impedance off 50 Ω. Prototypes showed ±8 Ω scatter — well outside the ±3 Ω specification. Root cause: cavity pressure variability during fill causing inconsistent pack-hold on PTFE.
Fixed
Mold tolerance ±0.05 mm + pack-hold profile + 100% TDR sort
Tightened mold tolerance on insulator inner diameter from ±0.2 mm to ±0.05 mm. Implemented optimized pack-hold profile via CAE cavity pressure analysis. Added 100% in-line TDR impedance measurement with automatic sorter. Final result: ±2.8 Ω — within spec, zero escapes.
Challenge 2
Assembly throughput — 40 units/min vs. 100+ required
Manual center-pin crimping capped at 40 units/min. Required 100+ units/min for 2 M annual volume. Contact resistance drift in 1.5% of manually crimped units due to inconsistent crimp force — high process variance unacceptable at volume.
Fixed
Pneumatic crimp fixture + machine-vision verification → 120 units/min
Designed pneumatic crimp tool integration fixture with machine-vision quality gate verifying crimp geometry after each cycle. Implemented automated cable stripping and positioning. Achieved 120 units/min at zero defect rate in pilot production — 3× manual throughput.

—Design Decisions & Lessons Learned
Problem 1
Information overload — 40% study completion rate
Initial prototype put all 35+ topics on a single page. Cognitive load was unmanageable during intensive study sessions. Users abandoned sessions mid-way through. Problem identical to poorly structured engineering documentation — too much information, no hierarchy.
Fixed
3-tier progressive disclosure model → 92% completion
Restructured into Fundamentals (5 topics) → Intermediate (8) → Expert (12). Tab-based UI for rapid switching. Each topic capped at 200–300 words with key takeaways highlighted. Next tier unlocks on completion. Completion rate: 40% → 92%.
Problem 2
Worst-case vs. RSS calculator — method confusion
Single-formula calculator produced output users couldn't interpret. No visual differentiation between worst-case sum and statistical RSS methods. Users selecting the wrong method for the wrong scenario — critical when sizing tolerance budgets on actual parts.
Fixed
Dual-mode toggle with side-by-side method comparison
Rebuilt calculator with toggle between Worst-Case and RSS modes. Added inline formula display. Implemented side-by-side comparison view with confidence indicators. Dynamic contributor stack with "+" button. Output verified against AIAG example problems.
Problem 3
Mobile unusable — nav occupied 40% of viewport
Navigation menu dominated phone screen real estate. Calculator inputs cramped below 480 px. Readable only on desktop — unusable during commute review sessions. Core use case (on-the-go study) was completely unsupported.
Fixed
Hamburger nav + vertically stacked inputs — fully functional below 480 px
Implemented hidden hamburger menu on mobile. Min 44 px touch targets (WCAG 2.5.5). Stacked calculator inputs vertically with larger input fields. CSS media queries for responsive font scaling. Tested on iPhone 12, Samsung S21, iPad — fully functional at 320 px wide.
