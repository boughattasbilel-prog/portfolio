# Confidential Y-Lock Connector Project

This project focused on improving the mechanical robustness, assembly reliability, and manufacturability of a W2B Positive Lock (PLK) Connector.
The public version of this case study is intentionally sanitized to avoid revealing company-sensitive drawings, internal part numbers, exact dimensions, or unreleased test data.

---

## Project Overview

- **Objective:** Strengthen the connector design so the locking system, pin interface, and assembly behavior remain reliable under automotive conditions.
  - Reduce risk of locking feature failure.
  - Improve pin alignment and contact stability.
  - Support manufacturing and assembly robustness.
  - Validate the design with engineering analysis and cross-functional review.

---

## What I Did

- Analyzed the weak points in the locking system and identified the main mechanical risks.
- Reviewed FEA results to confirm stress-sensitive areas.
- Proposed a material change from LCP to PBT 20% to improve structural behavior.
- Worked on pin geometry and pitch selection with electrical input to support the interface function.
- Coordinated design decisions to balance robustness, manufacturability, and production readiness.

---

## Tools & Methods

- **SolidWorks** for 3D CAD and assembly design.
- **FEA** for structural verification of critical features.
- **DFMEA** for failure-mode thinking and risk reduction.
- **Cross-functional collaboration** with electrical stakeholders.
- **Design reviews** to refine geometry and assembly behavior.

---

## Engineering Challenge

- The main challenge was that the secondary locking features were vulnerable to breakage under stress.
- This required a design response that improved strength without creating new issues in assembly, cost, or manufacturability.
- Pins alignment and contacts geometry also needed attention to make sure the mechanical design supported the electrical function.

---

## Result

- Improved the robustness of the locking architecture.
- Reduced the risk of mechanical failure in critical features.
- Supported a more reliable and manufacturable connector concept.
- Strengthened the project through simulation-based decisions and team collaboration.
